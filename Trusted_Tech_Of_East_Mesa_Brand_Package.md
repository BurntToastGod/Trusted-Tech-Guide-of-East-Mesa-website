## 1) Executive Recommendation
**Recommended final brand:** **Trusted Tech Of East Mesa**

- **Trusted** signals safety (passwords, accounts, scams) and puts families at ease.
- **Guide** signals teaching + confidence-building (not “repair shop,” not rushed).
- **East Mesa** makes it local, personal, and referral-friendly.
- The overall feel is calm, respectful, and easy to understand at a glance on a flyer.

**Core promise (1 sentence):**  
Patient, in-home and remote tech help that helps older adults and families feel confident and safe using phones, computers, Wi‑Fi, and online accounts—without jargon or pressure.

## 7) Typography (Senior-Friendly Pairing)
Choose fonts that are clear, not thin, and not condensed.

**Recommended pairing (Google Fonts):**

**Alternates (if you prefer):**
- Headlines: **Poppins** (600–700) or **Lato** (700)
- Body: **Open Sans** (400–600) or **Lato** (400–600)

**Basic rules:**
- Minimum body text size on print: **12–13 pt**
- Avoid ALL CAPS for paragraphs (harder to read)
- Keep line length short on flyers (easier scanning)

## 9) Brand Voice Guide (Do/Don’t + Examples)
### Voice traits
- Warm, calm, respectful
- Clear and practical
- Confidence-building (never shaming)
- Safety-minded (scams, privacy, accounts)
- Local and personal

### Do
- Use short sentences and simple words.
- Explain one step at a time.
- Use reassuring phrases: “We’ll do this together,” “No rush,” “You’re not alone in this.”
- Name the outcome: “So you can log in easily,” “So you know it’s safe.”

### Don’t
- Don’t imply people are incompetent.
- Don’t use jargon without a plain explanation.
- Don’t sound salesy or pushy.
- Don’t sound like a corporate IT provider.

### Example rewrites
- **Don’t:** “We fix user error and configuration issues.”  
  **Do:** “We’ll get your phone and Wi‑Fi working the way you want—and make sure you feel comfortable using it.”

- **Don’t:** “We provide cybersecurity services.”  
  **Do:** “We help you avoid scams, suspicious pop‑ups, and unsafe links—so you feel safer online.”

- **Don’t:** “Reset your credentials.”  
  **Do:** “We’ll help you reset your password and write it down in a safe way.”
